How to Create an Azure Account and Get $200 in Credits

Microsoft Azure is a comprehensive cloud computing platform offering a wide range of services for developers and businesses. Creating an Azure account is the first step towards leveraging the power of the cloud, and the good news is that Microsoft often provides generous credits for new users to explore Azure's capabilities. Let's dive into the steps to create an Azure account and claim your $200 in credits.

1. Visit the Azure Website

Go to the Microsoft Azure website and click on the "Start free" or "Create your free account" button. This will take you to the sign-up page.

2. Sign Up

On the sign-up page, enter your email address and choose a password for your Azure account. You will also be asked to select your country/region and agree to the terms and privacy statement. Click on "Next" to proceed.

3. Personal Information

In this step, you'll be asked to provide some personal information, such as your name, phone number, and country/region. Fill out the form accurately and click on "Next."

4. Payment Method

Azure will now ask you to provide a payment method. You can choose to add a credit card or use other available options, such as PayPal (where applicable). This is a necessary step to prevent fraud and ensure that you don't incur any accidental charges. However, you won't be charged during the free trial period.

5. Phone Verification

Azure may require you to verify your phone number. Enter your mobile phone number, and you will receive a verification code via SMS. Enter the code to complete the verification process.

6. Create a Subscription

After successfully signing up, you'll need to create a subscription. A subscription is like a billing plan that groups your services together. Click on "Start my free subscription" and choose the subscription type that suits your needs. For the free credits, you can select the "Pay-As-You-Go" subscription, which offers a $200 credit for 30 days.

7. Claim Your Credits

Once your subscription is active, you can start using your $200 in credits. Explore the Azure portal, deploy virtual machines, storage, databases, and more. Your credits will be automatically applied to your usage during the 30-day period.

8. Monitor Your Usage

Keep an eye on your Azure usage and spending. You can set up spending limits and alerts to ensure you don't exceed your credits. Navigate to the "Cost Management + Billing" section in the Azure portal to monitor your usage and manage your spending.

With your Azure account and credits, you're now ready to explore the vast array of services Azure has to offer. Remember to utilize the documentation, tutorials, and community resources available to make the most of your Azure journey.
